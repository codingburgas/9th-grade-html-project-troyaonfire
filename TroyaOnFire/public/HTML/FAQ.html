Fire prevention

How often should I check my smoke detectors?

Test your smoke detectors monthly by pressing the test button. Replace batteries at least once a year, or when the detector chirps to indicate low battery. Replace the entire smoke detector every 10 years.

What are the most common causes of house fires?

The most common causes include: cooking accidents (leading cause), heating equipment malfunctions, electrical issues, smoking materials, and candles. Always supervise cooking, maintain heating systems, and never leave candles unattended.

How can I make my home more fire-safe?

Install smoke detectors on every level, create and practice an escape plan, keep fire extinguishers in key areas (kitchen, garage), maintain electrical systems, clean dryer vents regularly, and store flammable materials safely.

Emergency Response

What should I do if there's a fire in my home?

GET OUT immediately and call 112. Don't try to fight large fires yourself. If trapped, close doors between you and the fire, seal cracks with cloth, and signal for help from a window. Crawl low under smoke.

When should I call the fire department?

Call 112 immediately for any fire that you cannot quickly extinguish with a small fire extinguisher, any fire involving electrical equipment, gas, or chemicals, or if you smell gas or suspect a gas leak.

How do I use a fire extinguisher properly?

Remember PASS: Pull the pin, Aim at the base of the fire, Squeeze the handle, Sweep from side to side. Only use on small fires. If the fire doesn't go out quickly, evacuate immediately.

Community Safety

How can I report a fire hazard in my neighborhood?

Use our fire map feature to report hazards, contact your local fire department directly, or call the non-emergency number. Provide specific location details and describe the hazard clearly.

What should I do if I see a wildfire starting?

Call 112 immediately with the exact location. Don't attempt to fight it yourself. If you're in danger, evacuate immediately. Follow local evacuation orders and routes.

How can I get involved in community fire safety?

Join our platform to share safety tips, attend local fire department events, volunteer for community safety programs, and help educate neighbors about fire prevention.
